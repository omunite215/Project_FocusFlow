# Evidence-Based Study Strategies for ADHD Students

## Active Recall

Active recall is the most effective study technique for ADHD students because it provides immediate feedback and dopamine reward:

- **What it is**: Testing yourself on material rather than passively re-reading.
- **How to use it**: Close the textbook, write down everything you remember, then check. The struggle to remember is where learning happens.
- **ADHD advantage**: The challenge and immediate feedback loop provides enough stimulation to maintain focus.
- **Optimal intervals**: Test yourself after each 15-20 minute study block.
- **Tools**: Flashcards (Anki), practice problems, teaching concepts to an imaginary student, writing from memory.

## Spaced Repetition

Spaced repetition fights the forgetting curve by reviewing material at optimal intervals:

- **Day 1**: Learn new material. Review same day before sleep.
- **Day 3**: First spaced review. This is where most forgetting occurs.
- **Day 7**: Second review. Material starts consolidating.
- **Day 14**: Third review. Long-term memory begins forming.
- **Day 30**: Final review for durable retention.
- **ADHD adaptation**: Use digital tools (Anki, Quizlet) that automate scheduling — don't rely on remembering to review.

## Pomodoro Adaptations for ADHD

The standard Pomodoro (25 min work / 5 min break) often fails ADHD students because:

- 25 minutes may be too long for low-interest subjects.
- Fixed intervals don't account for varying energy and focus levels.
- The pressure of a rigid timer can trigger anxiety.

### Adaptive Pomodoro for ADHD:
- **High energy + high interest**: 30-45 minute blocks, 5-minute breaks.
- **High energy + low interest**: 20-25 minute blocks, 5-minute breaks.
- **Low energy + high interest**: 20-30 minute blocks, 10-minute breaks.
- **Low energy + low interest**: 10-15 minute blocks, 10-minute breaks.
- **Key principle**: Match block length to current state, not a fixed rule.

## Body Doubling

Body doubling is studying in the presence of another person (or virtual presence):

- The presence of others provides external accountability and dopamine.
- Even silent co-working is effective — the other person doesn't need to help.
- Virtual body doubling (study streams, Discord study rooms) works nearly as well.
- FocusFlow check-ins serve a similar function — the periodic "someone is watching" effect.

## Task Chunking

Large tasks trigger executive function paralysis. Breaking them into tiny steps removes the activation energy barrier:

- **Bad**: "Study for organic chemistry exam" (too vague, too large).
- **Good**: "Read section 4.2 on nucleophilic substitution" → "Do practice problems 1-5" → "Write summary of key mechanisms."
- **Rule of thumb**: Each chunk should take 10-20 minutes maximum.
- **Start with the easiest chunk** to build momentum (the ADHD brain needs a "win" to activate).

## Subject Switching Strategies

For ADHD students, switching subjects can be a powerful tool OR a distraction:

### When to switch (productive):
- Focus has dropped below 2/5 for two consecutive check-ins.
- You've been on the same subject for more than 30 minutes and focus is declining.
- The current subject is low-interest and you have high-interest material available.

### When NOT to switch (avoidance):
- Switching to avoid difficult material you haven't attempted yet.
- Switching more than 3 times per hour (context-switching cost exceeds benefit).
- Switching during a focused flow state (check focus level first).

### Optimal subject order:
1. Start with medium-difficulty, medium-interest subject to warm up (5-15 min).
2. Move to the hardest/most important subject during peak focus.
3. End with the easiest or most enjoyable subject as a reward.

## The Two-Minute Rule

If a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it immediately:

- Eliminates task buildup in working memory.
- Provides quick dopamine hits that build momentum.
- Applies to study prep: "open the textbook," "find the practice problems," "set up the workspace."

## Environment Design for ADHD Study

The physical environment is a critical external support:

- **Minimize visual clutter**: Clear desk, put phone in another room.
- **Use noise strategically**: White noise, brown noise, or lo-fi music can improve focus. Silence is often worse.
- **Change locations**: Novel environments can restore focus when it drops. Study in different spots within a library.
- **Temperature and lighting**: Cooler temperatures and natural light support alertness.
- **Single-tasking setup**: Close all browser tabs except what's needed. Use website blockers.

## Study Method Matching

Different subjects benefit from different study methods:

| Subject Type | Best Methods | Why |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| Memorization-heavy (biology, vocabulary) | Flashcards, spaced repetition, mnemonics | Repetition strengthens retrieval pathways |
| Problem-solving (math, physics, CS) | Practice problems, worked examples, teaching | Procedural memory requires doing, not reading |
| Conceptual (philosophy, theory) | Mind mapping, Feynman technique, discussion | Understanding requires connecting ideas |
| Reading-heavy (history, literature) | SQ3R method, annotation, summarization | Active engagement prevents passive re-reading |
| Writing-intensive (essays, reports) | Outlining, freewriting, reverse outlining | Breaks the executive function barrier of "starting" |

## The Feynman Technique

Particularly effective for ADHD students because it's active and engaging:

1. Choose a concept you need to learn.
2. Explain it in simple language as if teaching a 12-year-old.
3. Identify gaps — where did your explanation break down?
4. Go back to the source material and fill the gaps.
5. Repeat until you can explain it clearly without notes.

## Movement Integration

Physical movement improves cognitive function, especially for ADHD:

- **Between blocks**: 5-minute walk, stretching, jumping jacks.
- **During study**: Standing desk, pacing while reviewing flashcards, using a balance board.
- **Exercise before studying**: 20-30 minutes of moderate exercise can improve focus for 1-2 hours.
- **Types of movement breaks**: Walking (best for processing information), stretching (best for physical tension), dancing (best for mood boost).
